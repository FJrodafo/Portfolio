<summary>連絡先 (れんらくさき / Renrakusaki): The place to contact someone, contact information.</summary>

1. 連 (れん / Ren): To connect, to link, to join...
    - This kanji indicates the idea of ​​connection or union between people.
    - It appears in words related to communication: 連絡 (れんらく / Renraku): Contact, communication...
2. 絡 (らく / Raku): Entwine, to coil...
    - It literally suggests a "link between people".
    - It complements 連 to form 連絡 (れんらく / Renraku): Contact, communication...
3. 先 (さき / Saki): Point, destination, place...
    - Here you indicate the place or address to which the communication is sent.
</details>

<summary>言語切替 (げんごきりかえ / Gengo kirikae): Change/toggle language.</summary>

1. 言 (げん / Gen): Word, say...
    - It indicates something related to language or communication.
2. 語 (ご / Go): Speech, language...
    - Combined 言語 (げんご / Gengo) literally means "language".
3. 切替 (きりかえ / Kirikae): Exchange, replacement, switching to...
    - Composed of:
        - 切 (きり / Kiri): Cut, cut of, be sharp...
        - 替 (かえ / Kae): Exchange, spare, substitute...
</details>
<details>
<summary>開発者 (かいはつしゃ / Kaihatsusha): Developer, someone who develops something.</summary>

1. 開 (かい / Kai): Open, start, unfold, unseal...
    - It refers to opening something physically (a door, window, box) or figuratively (a meeting, an event, development, opportunities).
2. 発 (はつ / Hatsu): Start from, publish, launch, release...
    - It often conveys the idea of something starting, being sent out, or being generated, whether physically (a rocket, a signal) or abstractly (a trend, a speech, a plan).
3. 者 (しゃ / Sha): Person, someone...
    - It means person, but unlike 人 (ひと / Hito) it is usually used for professional contexts, that is, people who practice a profession.
</details>
<details>
<summary>音楽家 (おんがくか / Ongakuka): Musician.</summary>

1. 音 (おん / On): Sound, noise...
    - It generally means sound, but in musical contexts it can refer to a musical note or musical pitch.
2. 楽 (がく / Gaku): Music.
    - It can mean:
        - Music when read as がく as in 音楽 (おんがく / Ongaku).
        - Enjoyable/Fun when read as たの as in 楽しい (たのしい / Tanoshī).
3. 家 (か / Ka): Professional, expert, performer...
    - A person who specializes in or is professionally engaged in something.
</details>
<details>
<summary>諸島 (しょとう / Shotō): Archipelago, group of islands.</summary>

1. 諸 (しょ / Sho): Various, many, several...
    - It is used to indicate a set or plurality of elements.
2. 島 (とう / Tō): Island.
    - Concept associated with a land surrounded by water and separated from the continent.
    - It can be used both literally and metaphorically, something isolated or independent.
</details>
<details>
<summary>出身 (しゅっしん / Shusshin): Descent, extraction, origin...</summary>

1. 出 (しゅつ / Shutsu): Come out, leave...
    - It mainly indicates leaving but is mainly used in combination with other kanji, 出る (でる / Deru) for example is the verb to leave, attend, appear...
2. 身 (しん / Shin): Somebody, person, one's station in life...
    - It means physical body, oneself or person and personal state or situation.
</details>
<details>
<summary>奏者 (そうしゃ / Sōsha): Performer, a professional musician who plays an instrument.</summary>

1. 奏 (そう / Sō): Play music...
    - It is mainly used to indicate the action of playing a musical instrument in a professional or formal manner.
2. 者 (しゃ / Sha): Person, someone...
    - It means person, but unlike 人 (ひと / Hito) it is usually used for professional contexts, that is, people who practice a profession.
</details>
<details>
<summary>送る (おく.る / Okuru): To send, to dispatch, to forward, to transmit, to ship, to remit...</summary>

- It is used to express the action of sending something, whether tangible or intangible.
</details>
<details>
<summary>下 (した / Shita): Down, below, under...</summary>

- It indicates a lower position or a downward movement.
</details>

<details>
<summary>両方 (りょうほう / Ryōhō): Both, both sides, both parties...</summary>

1. 両 (りょう / Ryō): Both... 
   - It conveys duality or the totality of two elements.
2. 方 (ほう / Hō): Direction, side, method...  
   - Conveys the idea of orientation, direction, or the manner of doing something.   
</details>
<details>
<summary>設計 (せっけい / Sekkei): Plan, design, layout...</summary>

1. 設 (せつ / Setsu): Establishment, provision, prepare  
   - Representing creating, establishing, or planning something deliberately and in an organized way, often in engineering, construction, or system contexts.
2. 計 (けい / Kei): Plot, plan, scheme, measure...  
   - A series of steps to be carried out or goals to be accomplished.   
</details>
<details>
<summary>包括的 (ほうかつてき / hōkatsu-teki): Comprehensive, inclusive, encompassing...</summary>

1. 包 (ほう / Hō): To wrap, include, enclose... 
   - Is the idea of wrapping, including, or encompassing, often used in words that imply completeness or coverage.
2. 括 (かつ / Katsu): To tie together, to bundle, to encompass...  
   - Conveys the sense of grouping, summarizing, or including collectively.
   - Part of 包括 (hōkatsu) → “to encompass, to include all”  
3. 的 (てき / Teki)
   - Functions as an adjectival suffix, similar to “-ic,” “-ive,” or “-al”.
   - Turns a noun or concept into a descriptive adjective.     
</details>
<details>
<summary>満足のいく (まんぞくのいく / manzoku no iku): Satisfying, pleasing, gratifying...</summary>

1. 満 (まん / Man): Full, fullness, enough... 
   - Conveys the sense of having reached capacity or totality.
2. 足 (そく / Soku): Leg, foot, be suffiecient...  
   - “Foot” literally, but also sufficient, enough, adequate in compound words.   
</details>
<details>
<summary>提供 (ていきょう / teikyō): Offer, tender, supplying...</summary>

1. 提 (てい / Tei): Propose, Take along, Carry in hand... 
   - Represents the action of bringing something toward someone or putting it forward.
2. 供 (きょう / Kyō): To serve, supply, give, offer...  
   - Conveys the idea of providing something to someone, often in a supportive or service-oriented sense.  
</details>
<details>
<summary>履歴書 (りれきしょ / Rirekisho): Curriculum Vitae...</summary>

1. 履 (り / Ri): Complete, to follow a path... 
   - Conveys the idea of progress, journey, or trajectory.
2. 歴 (れき / Reki): History, record, experience, chronological account...  
   - Represents a sequence of events or a documented track of past actions. 
   - Combined with 履 (ri) → 履歴 = “history or record of one’s path”.
3. 書 (しょ / Sho): Document, write, book, writing... 
   - Shows writing, documentation, or recording.    
</details>
